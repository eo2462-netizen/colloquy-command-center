The Core Valuation Formula
Catalog Value = TTM Net Revenue (NPS or NLS) × Market Multiple
Your thesis: if TTM includes a non-recurring exploitation spike, the resulting catalog value is overstated by exactly (spike revenue × multiple). Kate Bush example: if $2.3M/month spike was annualized = $27.6M/yr. At 16x = $441.6M implied value. Real baseline ~$480K/yr = $7.7M. Distortion = $433.9M. That's the stakes.
Scipio's Debt Upsize Formula
New Debt Capacity = New Appraised Value × LTV%
Incremental Proceeds = New Debt Capacity − Existing Debt
Example: $100M catalog at 65% LTV = $65M debt. Exploitation events grow value to $150M. New capacity = $97.5M. Incremental = $32.5M. This can be used to buy more catalogs or pay a dividend — without selling anything. If the $50M value increase was exploitation-driven and reverts, the catalog is now over-leveraged.
ABS vs. Bank Debt — WACC Comparison (Shot Tower p.18)
Bank Debt: WACC = 9.51% (55% LTV, 6.8% cost of debt)
ABS: WACC = 8.38% (65% LTV, 6.5% cost of debt)
1.13% difference allows ABS buyer to pay ~10% more for the same asset while maintaining the same 14.9% equity return. This means inflated TTM revenue × lower ABS discount rate = structurally higher transaction prices. Distortion is amplified in ABS environments.

Vocabulary & Cheat Sheet

Every term you need to own. Know these cold before any industry conversation.

TLDR — The 6 terms your thesis lives or dies on: TTM (trailing 12-month revenue — where the distortion enters) · NPS/NLS (the cash flow metrics multiples are applied to) · Step change vs. spike (the central question your empirical work answers) · ABS (why valuation accuracy matters beyond just sale price) · LTV (the lever that connects inflated valuation to downstream financing risk) · Normalization (the best practice gap your thesis fills). If you can explain all six to a non-expert in 60 seconds each, you're ready to write.

TTM / Trailing Twelve Months
The last 12 months of revenue used as the base for valuation multiples. Central to your thesis — exploitation events that hit within this window directly inflate the calculated catalog value.
NPS — Net Publisher's Share
Net income retained by a music publisher after paying the songwriter's share. Current 2024 benchmark: 16.1x NPS (ex-iconic), 17.5x including iconic transactions. The primary cash flow metric for publishing catalog valuation.
NLS — Net Label Share
Net income retained by a record label after artist royalties. Current 2024 benchmark: 13.0x NLS (ex-iconic), 14.2x including iconic. Always trades at ~20% discount to NPS due to higher exploitation costs and less diversified income streams.
DCF — Discounted Cash Flow
Estimates present value of all future expected cash flows, discounted by WACC. Best practice per Alderbrook (2022): apply DCF alongside multiples, normalize one-off events. Requires projecting forward — doesn't assume past = future. Discount rate for stable catalog: ~8.25–9.5%.
WACC — Weighted Avg. Cost of Capital
Blended cost of a company's debt and equity financing. Used as DCF discount rate. ABS financing lowers WACC by ~1.13% vs. bank debt (Shot Tower p.18), allowing buyers to pay ~10% more for the same asset while maintaining target equity returns.
ABS — Asset-Backed Securitization
Financing structure pooling royalty-generating catalogs as collateral for notes sold to institutional investors. Rated by Kroll, Moody's. Music ABS surged 2022–2024: $3B+ raised in 2024 alone. Apollo, PIMCO, Nuveen are major investors. Typical deal size: $80M–$1.8B.
LTV — Loan-to-Value Ratio
Debt as % of appraised asset value. Music ABS: 52–65% LTV. Covenants require maintaining LTV below threshold. If appraised value rises from exploitation, company can upsize debt. If value later falls, covenant breach risk. This is Scipio's $100M → $150M example.
Step Change
A permanent upward shift in streaming performance following an exploitation event — the new baseline is sustainably higher. Represents legitimate durable value creation. Your thesis verdict for ABBA. The key question your empirical work answers for each case study.
Spike / Reversion
A temporary surge in streaming that eventually returns to pre-event baseline. Creates TTM revenue distortion if it falls within the valuation window. Should be normalized per best practice — but often isn't in multiples-based approaches.
Normalization
Adjusting historical revenue to remove one-off or non-recurring items before applying multiples or building DCF projections. Alderbrook (2022) specifically recommends normalizing "unusually high sync" income. The gap between best practice (normalize) and common practice (don't) is the core problem your thesis addresses.
Exploitation Event
Any event that generates renewed attention to an existing catalog: sync placement, TikTok virality, biopic, anniversary campaign, artist death, remix. Can be positive (revenue-generating) or negative (reputational). Your study covers four types with distinct durability patterns.
Kroll Bond Rating Agency (KBRA)
Third-party valuation and ratings agency for music ABS. Applied 8.25% discount rate to stable catalog (Kobalt deal); 11.75% for newer songs. Their appraisals determine ABS debt capacity — making accurate TTM revenue inputs critical to appropriate leverage levels.
Iconic Premium
Valuation premium for culturally significant catalogs (Dylan, Springsteen, MJ, Queen). Multiples 18–30x+ vs. 16x standard. Shot Tower (2025) notes premiums reflect perceived durability — which your thesis tests empirically: are iconic-level streaming numbers always durable?
Deep Catalog
Luminate definition: music streamed 5+ years after release. Kate Bush, Fleetwood Mac, and ABBA all qualify. Rock is most deep-catalog-heavy genre (72.6% of streams from deep catalog). Low baseline streaming rates make exploitation spikes dramatically large in % terms.
Qualitative Comparative Case Study
Your methodological approach. Analyzes multiple cases to identify patterns, not to test a hypothesis or prove causation. Appropriate when the goal is exploratory documentation rather than statistical inference. Explicitly: no econometrics, no causal claims.
SOFR — Secured Overnight Financing Rate
Base interest rate used in music catalog financing (replaced LIBOR). Current music ABS spreads: +185 to +250 basis points over 5-year treasury. Relevant because ABS base rate directly affects the discount rate used in Kroll appraisals supporting your case study catalog valuations.
